ACT III OVERVIEW (DM SUMMARY)

The moment the keystone is disturbed, Kethrys stops slipping quietly.

The castle lurches violently between planes. Walls grind. Floors tilt. The containment protocols fail. Something awakens.

The Planar Warden begins moving toward the party immediately.

This act is not about exploration. It is about escape under pursuit.

THE AWAKENING (READ OR PARAPHRASE)

The instant the keystone gives way, the chamber shudders.

Stone screams against stone. Light fractures across the walls. Somewhere deep within Kethrys, something moves with purpose for the first time in centuries.

A presence surges toward you.

Not hunting.
Not raging.
Correcting.

The castle lurches again, harder this time, phasing violently in and out of alignment.

You have moments.

THE WARDEN (DM SUMMARY)

  • The Planar Warden is bound to Kethrys
  • It awakens when containment fails
  • It does not speak
  • It does not stop

It will:

  • Pursue relentlessly
  • Ignore lesser obstacles
  • Be delayed, not defeated, inside the castle

Killing it inside Kethrys is not the goal.

CHASE STRUCTURE (KEEP THIS SIMPLE)

This is a narrative chase, not a tactical dungeon crawl.

Run it as:

  • A sequence of fast decisions
  • One roll per character per major obstacle
  • Failure costs time, position, or HP

Advance the dungeon clock aggressively here.

AVAILABLE ESCAPE ROUTES

Present two clear options quickly.

OPTION 1: THE ORIGINAL ENTRYWAY

The path back through the Courtyard aligns briefly.

Pros:

  • Familiar route
  • Fastest escape

Cons:

  • The Warden can follow
  • Least cover during pursuit

This is the clean escape.

OPTION 2: THE SERVICE TRAPDOOR

A secondary route reveals itself as the castle shudders.

  • A narrow maintenance passage
  • Collapsing
  • Too small for the Warden to pass fully through

Pros:

  • Temporarily blocks pursuit
  • Buys time

Cons:

  • Dangerous descent
  • Scattered exit location outside
  • The Warden will still emerge later

This is the desperate escape.

RUNNING THE CHASE (DM TOOLS)

Use escalating descriptions:

  • Floors tilting mid-stride
  • Walls phasing into obstacles
  • Gravity surging unpredictably

When momentum slows:

  • Advance the clock
  • Bring the Warden closer
  • Collapse something behind them

Do not stall.

FAILURE DURING THE CHASE

Failure should:

  • Separate the party briefly
  • Inflict damage or exhaustion
  • Cost equipment or positioning

Never stop the chase entirely.

The castle does not wait.

EXITING KETHRYS

Once the party clears either route:

  • Kethrys phases violently behind them
  • The exit snaps shut or tears free
  • The Warden follows moments later, partially manifested

This leads directly into the outside confrontation.

ACT III END CONDITION

Act III ends when:

  • The party escapes the castle
  • The Warden fully emerges outside
  • The environment stabilizes enough for a final stand

From here, initiative may finally be rolled.

DM MINDSET FOR ACT III

You are not adjudicating movement.
You are maintaining pressure.

If players stop moving, the castle moves for them.

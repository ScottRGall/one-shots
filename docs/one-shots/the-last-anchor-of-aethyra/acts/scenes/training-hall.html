Training Hall

Training Hall

Role: High-risk zone, consequence amplifier
Risk Level: High
Accessibility: Phases in and out
Design Note: This room is intentionally more dangerous than others

FIRST IMPRESSION (READ OR PARAPHRASE)

The training hall opens into a wide, circular chamber scored with weapon marks and scorched stone. Racks of practice weapons line the walls, some cracked, some half-phased into the stone itself. Faded sigils are carved into the floor in overlapping patterns.

As you step inside, the air tightens.

For a split second, you see figures moving at the edges of your vision. Blades raised. Spells half-cast.

Then they are gone.

IMMEDIATE PLAYER TAKEAWAYS

Make these clear quickly:

  • This room feels active, not abandoned
  • The air vibrates with stored motion
  • This is not a safe place to linger
  • Being alone here would be dangerous

You do not need to say why. The room communicates it.

WHAT THE ROOM IS DOING (DM ONLY)

The Training Hall is replaying fragments of past drills, duels, and spell exercises as planar echoes.

These echoes:

  • Do not think
  • Do not pursue intelligently
  • React violently to movement and presence

They are not monsters. They are residual force.

GROUP PRESENCE (PARTY TOGETHER)

If the party enters together:

  • Echoes flicker but do not fully manifest
  • Movement triggers near misses
  • The danger is real but manageable

Suggested Check (Optional):
Dexterity or Acrobatics DC 12 to avoid incidental damage while moving quickly.

Failure:
Minor damage or loss of footing.

This room punishes hesitation, not teamwork.

SOLO ENTRY OR ISOLATION (HIGH RISK)

If a character is alone here during a phase shift, or enters alone:

State: Planar Pocket

Past training bouts fully replay.

Blades swing. Spells discharge. The room does not slow down for you.

Solo Challenge:
Dexterity save DC 14

On Failure (choose one):

  • Meaningful damage
  • Knocked prone and disoriented
  • Gain one level of exhaustion

A second failure escalates consequences.

This is the clearest signal in Act II that splitting up has teeth.

IF THE PARTY LINGERS

Every round or extended pause here risks escalation.

Options:

  • Advance the dungeon clock
  • Increase echo intensity
  • Force a choice to move or take damage

Do not allow this room to become a puzzle.

PHASING CUES

Before the Training Hall phases out:

  • The echoes speed up
  • Weapon impacts overlap
  • The sigils on the floor glow unevenly

If players ignore the warning, consequences follow.

EXITING THE TRAINING HALL

Leaving feels like stepping out of a strong current.

Once exited:

  • The echoes fade abruptly
  • Silence feels heavy by contrast
  • The party understands this place was not meant to be safe

SCENE END CONDITION

The scene ends when:

  • The party moves on quickly
  • The room phases out
  • The danger has made its point

This room does not need to be revisited.

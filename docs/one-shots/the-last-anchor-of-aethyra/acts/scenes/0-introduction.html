Introduction – The Last Anchor of Aethyra

Scene Purpose (GM Only)

Establish inevitability, define success correctly, and secure player consent to time pressure and loss.

  • Reveals: The castle cannot be stopped, only shaped
  • Applies pressure: Limited time, irreversible outcomes
  • Taxes: Certainty, safety, and the expectation of a clean victory

If this scene is skipped, players will misinterpret later constraints as arbitrary or unfair.

Entry Trigger

The scene begins when:

  • Play starts
  • The party is summoned by their patron

This scene cannot be bypassed.

If players attempt to derail or delay:

  • Thalos proceeds anyway
  • The situation does not improve

Immediate Impression (Read Aloud)

Thalos summons you without ceremony. Not to a council chamber or study, but to a space already prepared for departure. The air hums faintly with restrained magic. He looks tired in a way you have never seen before.

Scene State

  • The party is safe
  • The threat is distant but already in motion
  • Time pressure exists but has not yet begun ticking visibly
  • This is the last calm moment

What is not happening yet:

  • No pursuit
  • No collapse
  • No immediate danger

Interactive Elements

  • Sealed Letter

    • Appears mundane
    • Warm with residual magic
    • Contains the full written briefing

  • Thalos’s Spellwork

    • Already prepared
    • Cannot be meaningfully delayed

Failure Mode:

  • Attempting to over-question changes nothing except tone

Actors Present

  • Archwizard Thalos Aethyrian
    • Attitude: Calm, exhausted, precise
    • Wants: Damage contained, not heroics
    • Changes behavior if: The party refuses outright

If the party refuses:

  • Thalos does not argue
  • He departs
  • The disaster unfolds unchecked

Hidden Information (GM Only)

These facts are not debated and are delivered as certainty:

  • Kethrys is already in final planar transit
  • Five of its six anchors are already gone
  • No remaining magic can stop the castle’s departure

This information is delivered in writing, not explained aloud.

What Thalos Says Aloud (Read or Paraphrase)

Thalos does not lecture. He does not speculate.

He hands the sealed letter to the party and says:

“You will not stop it. You will only decide how it leaves.”

He pauses briefly, as if considering saying more, then adds:

“Read this. There is no time.” Assets/The Last Anchor of Aethyra - Mission Letter.png

The spell completes before questions can be asked.

Mission Truths (Delivered via the Letter)

The letter states, plainly:

  • Kethrys was once stabilized by six planar anchors
  • Five have already failed or been destroyed
  • One final keystone remains within the castle
  • If the castle departs with the keystone intact, the anchor will tear free, causing a spreading planar rupture across the region
  • If the keystone is removed or destroyed before transit completes, the rupture will still occur but will collapse inward, localizing the damage to the castle’s exit point
  • Retrieval is preferred, destruction is acceptable
  • The keystone is unstable and may resist interference
  • Survival matters; nothing is gained if the party is lost

There is no alternative plan.

Player Decision Hooks

Explicit choices:

  • Accept the task
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Attempt to negotiate terms

If players hesitate or stall:

  • Thalos ends the conversation
  • The transition proceeds anyway

Pressure and Escalation

Escalation here is narrative, not mechanical.

Track:

  1. Calm explanation
  2. Clear inevitability
  3. Immediate transition

Escalation advances when:

  • Questions loop
  • Players attempt to delay commitment

Stakes

If the players accept:

  • They enter the dungeon with correct expectations
  • They understand success is containment, not victory

If they refuse:

  • The rupture occurs unchecked
  • The adventure ends early and grimly

Avoidance is possible, but the cost is global.

Exit Paths

  • Forced Planar Transition
    • The world folds
    • The party is relocated near Kethrys mid-transit

There is no return to normalcy after this.

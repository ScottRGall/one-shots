Epilogue

Epilogue – The Patron Arrives

Scene Purpose (GM Only)

Provide authoritative closure, validate the party’s actions, and frame consequences through an external lens without diminishing player agency.

If mishandled, the Patron becomes a lecture. Keep them brief and pointed.

Entry Trigger

The epilogue begins when:

  • The Final Confrontation ends
  • The castle’s fate is sealed
  • Immediate danger has passed

Cut hard. No slow fade.

Expanded Opening Beat – The End of Kethrys (Read Aloud)

The ground shudders one final time, then stills.

Behind you, what remains of Kethrys does not explode. It does not collapse outward. Instead, the ruin folds inward on itself, stone and light bending as if drawn toward an unseen center. Towers shear away into fragments of pale geometry, their edges dissolving before they can strike the ground.

The sound is wrong. Not thunder. Not silence. A deep, steady pressure that fades as the castle shrinks, pulling its own shadow with it.

For a moment, you can see through the structure. Hallways overlap. Courtyards hang at impossible angles. Rooms you passed through flicker into view and are gone again, like memories slipping away.

Then there is a final, sharp contraction.

Kethrys vanishes.

What remains is empty ground, scorched stone, and a faint distortion in the air that does not fully unwind. Near the center of the ruin, space itself seems thin, like glass stretched too far. Light bends subtly around it. Sound dulls. Standing too close makes your skin prickle and your breath catch, as though your body knows it does not quite belong there.

The sky above snaps back into place. Wind returns. Sound returns. The world feels heavier. Real.

The tear does not close. It only quiets.

Scene State

  • Reality has stabilized
  • The party is safe, but altered
  • Time has resumed its normal flow

This is a moment of assessment, not threat.

The Patron Appears

Patron Arrival – (Read Aloud)

The air nearby tightens.

Not violently. Precisely.

Space folds along a single, clean line, as if reality itself has been carefully opened rather than torn. There is no flash, no thunder, no surge of magic. One moment the space is empty. The next, someone is standing there.

He has not arrived in haste. His boots rest firmly on the ground, as though it had been waiting for him. The faint distortion around him collapses inward and is gone, leaving the world abruptly ordinary again.

The man’s robes are travel-worn rather than ceremonial, layered with sigils that are faded from use, not neglect. His hair is threaded with grey that no glamour bothers to hide. His presence is not overwhelming, but it is absolute.

He does not look at the ruins where the castle stood. He does not survey the horizon. His eyes settle on you immediately, sharp and assessing, as though he has been watching from much farther away.

For a brief moment, his expression tightens. Not fear. Not anger. Relief, carefully restrained.

“It is done,” he says at last, his voice calm and exact. “And you are still standing.”

The Patron Speaks (Read or Paraphrase)

Keep this to three beats, no more.

Beat 1 – Acknowledgment

“You held what should not have been held. Most would have fled. You did not.”

Beat 2 – Judgment

“The cost was never going to be zero. What matters is that the breach did not spread.”

Beat 3 – Consequence

“What happens next is not mine to decide alone. But neither is it forgotten.”

Do not explain further unless asked.

Outcome Framing (GM Only)

Choose the Patron’s tone based on the party’s performance:

Clean Success

  • Calm approval
  • Minimal commentary
  • Subtle respect

Costly Success

  • Acknowledgment of sacrifice
  • No consolation
  • Clear gravity

Pyrrhic Survival

  • Unease
  • Warning rather than praise
  • Hints of future consequence

The Patron never lies. They also never comfort.

World Consequences (Patron Framed)

Have the Patron reference 2 concrete outcomes:

  • The fate of Kethrys
  • The wider planar balance
  • Who will now notice the party

Each is one sentence. No lore dump.

Player Response Window (Important)

Give the players space.

Ask one open question:

  • “Do you answer?”
  • “Do you ask anything?”
  • “Do you say nothing?”

Let each character respond briefly or choose silence.

Silence is a valid choice.

Character Closure

After the Patron departs, go around the table.

For each character:

  • One sentence on how this encounter changes them
  • Or one decision they make moving forward

Do not allow cross-talk here.

Patron Departure

Describe the Patron leaving:

  • No flash
  • No portal
  • No spectacle

They are simply no longer there.

The world feels smaller without them.

Final Image (Read Aloud)

End on a grounded image, not the Patron.

Examples:

  • Dawn over broken stone
  • The road away from Kethrys
  • A quiet moment tending wounds

Then stop.

Scene End Condition

The session ends when:

  • Every player has had one closing moment
  • The Patron has departed
  • The final image is delivered

Dice down. Session over.

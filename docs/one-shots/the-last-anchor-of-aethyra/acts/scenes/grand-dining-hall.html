Grand Dining Hall

Role: False refuge, time sink, atmospheric pressure
Risk Level: Low to Medium
Accessibility: Phases in and out

FIRST IMPRESSION (READ OR PARAPHRASE)

You step into a vast dining hall lined with long stone tables and high-backed chairs. The space is intact in a way much of the castle is not. Banners hang unmoving along the walls. Plates, goblets, and serving dishes sit where they were last placed.

For a moment, everything feels still.

Then a chair scrapes across the floor with no one touching it.
A goblet tips, falls, and shatters.
The sound echoes twice, slightly out of time.

IMMEDIATE PLAYER TAKEAWAYS

Make these clear early:

  • This room feels more stable than most
  • The air is quiet, heavy, and muted
  • No immediate threat is visible
  • Time feels slightly stretched here

This room invites the party to pause.

WHAT THE ROOM IS DOING (DM ONLY)

The Dining Hall exists slightly out of sync with the rest of Kethrys.

  • Sounds lag or repeat
  • Movement feels slowed or oddly smooth
  • Planar echoes replay fragments of the past

Nothing here attacks on its own.

The cost of this room is delay.

IF THE PARTY LINGERS (OPTIONAL DETAILS)

Reveal one detail each time they hesitate or spend time here.

After a short pause:

  • The smell of food briefly fills the air, then vanishes.

After further delay:

  • Ghostly figures flicker at the tables, mid-conversation, then disappear.

After notable hesitation:

  • Cutlery rattles. One table phases halfway into the floor.
  • The dungeon clock advances.

Make it clear: staying here feels safe, but it is not free.

INTERACTION OPTIONS

Players may:

  • Search for supplies or clues
  • Attempt a short rest
  • Observe planar echoes
  • Regroup and plan

Suggested Checks (Optional):

  • Investigation or Perception DC 12 to notice repeating echoes
  • Insight DC 12 to realize the room distorts time slightly

Success provides information, not advantage.

SOLO RISK (IF ALONE DURING PHASE SHIFT)

Use the Solo Phase Risk Table.

Dining Hall Result:
Disorientation from looping echoes.

Effect:
Disadvantage on the next roll or loss of time.

No combat. No major damage.

PHASING CUES

When this room is about to phase out:

  • Conversations echo backward
  • Banners ripple as if pulled by unseen wind
  • The far end of the hall blurs

Give at least one warning before alignment breaks.

EXITING THE DINING HALL

Leaving feels reluctant, like pushing through thick air.

When the party exits:

  • The room may remain visible briefly
  • Or fade immediately, depending on the dungeon clock

Either reinforces impermanence.

SCENE END CONDITION

The scene ends when:

  • The party leaves voluntarily
  • The room phases out
  • Pressure elsewhere pulls attention away

This room is never mandatory.

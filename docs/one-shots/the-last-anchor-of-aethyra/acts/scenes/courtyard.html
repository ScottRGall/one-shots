Courtyard

Role: Hub, orientation point, pressure amplifier
Risk Level: Low
Always Accessible: Yes

FIRST IMPRESSION (READ OR PARAPHRASE)

You emerge into a wide, open courtyard of cracked stone and fractured geometry. Above you, the sky is wrong. Clouds slide past in layers, some moving faster than others, some not moving at all. The air hums with a low vibration you feel in your teeth more than you hear.

At irregular intervals, parts of the castle around you flicker. Towers fade to translucent outlines. Walls briefly show what lies beyond them, then snap back into place.

Kethrys is not collapsing.

It is slipping away.

IMMEDIATE PLAYER TAKEAWAYS

Make these clear within the first minute:

  • This space feels relatively stable
  • Other rooms are visible only when aligned
  • Exits are archways, not corridors
  • Some archways are solid. Others are hazy or incomplete

Point out which archways are currently in phase. Make it obvious that others are not.

PHASED ARCHWAYS (IMPORTANT)

  • In-phase archways look solid and reachable
  • Phased-out archways appear as:
    • Faint outlines
    • Shimmering doorframes
    • Empty spaces where a passage should be

Players immediately understand:

“We can’t go everywhere right now.”

No roll required.

IF THE PARTY LINGERS (OPTIONAL DETAILS)

Reveal one detail every few minutes they remain here, or whenever they hesitate.

After a short pause:

  • Gravity subtly shifts. Small debris slides uphill.

After further delay:

  • The sound of distant stone grinding echoes, slightly out of sync.

After notable hesitation:

  • One visible archway flickers and phases out.
  • Another flickers into alignment elsewhere.

If they linger too long, advance the dungeon clock.

INTERACTION OPTIONS (LOW RISK)

The Courtyard supports:

  • Regrouping
  • Brief planning
  • Short rest attempts (at cost)
  • Waiting for alignment changes

Make it clear:

  • This space buys clarity, not safety
  • Nothing here stops the castle from moving

SOLO RISK (IF ALONE DURING PHASE SHIFT)

If a character is alone here during a shift:

  • Gravity lurches
  • Vision blurs

Suggested Check:
Dexterity or Constitution save DC 12

On Failure:
Knocked prone, minor damage, or momentary disorientation

DM TOOLS IN THIS SCENE

Use the Courtyard to:

  • Show the dungeon clock advancing
  • Swap which rooms are accessible
  • Telegraph danger without combat
  • Reunite separated characters

This is where players feel the rules of Kethrys most clearly.

EXITING THE COURTYARD

Leaving the Courtyard always feels risky.

Describe:

  • The momentary resistance of crossing thresholds
  • The sense of committing to a choice
  • The Courtyard shifting slightly once they leave

The castle notices movement.

SCENE END CONDITION

This scene never truly ends.

It recurs throughout Act II as:

  • The regroup point
  • The reference frame
  • The reminder that time is passing

EXIT RESTRICTION (DM ONLY)

Although the Courtyard remains stable, no true exit from Kethrys is accessible during Act II.

  • Any archway resembling the original entrance is incomplete or misaligned
  • Attempts to leave lead to:
    • A short loop back into the Courtyard
    • A brief planar slip that returns them elsewhere inside Kethrys
    • A sensation of resistance, as if the castle is no longer aligned with the outside world

Do not state this as a rule. Let players test it once if they try.

The exit does not fully align until the Throne Room becomes accessible and the castle reaches a later stage of transit.

HOW TO HANDLE PLAYER TESTING (IMPORTANT)

If a player says:

“Can we just leave?”

Respond with description, not mechanics:

  • “The archway looks solid for a moment, then folds back in on itself.”
  • “You step forward and feel pressure, like walking into a strong wind.”
  • “The view beyond the archway is not the field you entered from.”

No roll required. No damage. No lecture.

They learn:

“We are committed, whether we like it or not.”

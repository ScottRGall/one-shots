Guard Barracks

Guard Barracks

Role: Moral friction, social tension, imperfect allies
Risk Level: Medium
Accessibility: Phases in and out

FIRST IMPRESSION (READ OR PARAPHRASE)

The barracks are narrow and reinforced, with low stone ceilings and thick support beams. Weapon racks line the walls, many still holding rusted blades and cracked shields. Bedrolls lie where they were dropped long ago.

This place feels used.

A flicker of movement catches your eye. Someone is here, watching you from behind overturned bunks.

IMMEDIATE PLAYER TAKEAWAYS

Make these clear quickly:

  • This room feels more sheltered than most
  • It has been occupied recently
  • The occupants are alert, frightened, and armed
  • Violence here would be loud and costly

THE SCAVENGERS (DM SUMMARY)

A small group of scavengers has taken shelter here after entering Kethrys earlier. They know the castle is unstable. They want to leave. They also want what they came for.

They are not villains. They are desperate.

What they have:

  • Minor supplies
  • Partial knowledge of the castleâ€™s shifts
  • A lesser planar trinket they are unwilling to abandon

What they do not have:

  • A way out
  • Trust in strangers
  • Time to argue endlessly

PLAYER OPTIONS (ALL VALID)

1. Escort Them
The party agrees to bring the scavengers along.

  • Slows movement
  • Complicates future phase shifts
  • Creates later sacrifice or rescue moments

2. Ignore or Abandon Them
The party leaves them behind.

  • No immediate mechanical cost
  • Clear narrative consequence later
  • Scavengers may reappear or be referenced

3. Fight Them
The party uses force.

  • Costs time
  • Makes noise
  • Advances the dungeon clock

No option is framed as correct.

IF NEGOTIATION STALLS

If discussion drags:

  • A planar tremor shakes the room
  • One exit begins to blur
  • The dungeon clock advances

Pressure resolves indecision.

SOLO RISK (IF ALONE DURING PHASE SHIFT)

Use the Solo Phase Risk Table.

Barracks Result:
Isolation or confrontation.

Effect:

  • Threatening standoff
  • Loss of equipment
  • Minor damage
  • Or dungeon clock advance

No one is killed off-screen.

PHASING CUES

Before this room phases out:

  • Support beams creak
  • Light bends around doorframes
  • Sounds from elsewhere bleed in

Give a warning. Then move on.

EXITING THE BARRACKS

Leaving feels cramped and rushed.

If scavengers are with the party:

  • They hesitate
  • They argue
  • They slow decisions

If abandoned:

  • The room feels emptier than it should

Both outcomes matter later.

SCENE END CONDITION

The scene ends when:

  • The party commits to a choice
  • The room phases out
  • Time pressure overrides conversation

Keystone Chamber

The Keystone Chamber

Role: Climax, decision point, point of no return
Accessibility: Phase 3 only
Tone: Finality, pressure, containment rather than victory

GRAND ENTRY DESCRIPTION

(Read or paraphrase)

Beyond the throne, the world narrows.

You step into a chamber carved from a single sweep of stone, vast and impossibly precise. The walls curve inward, not to enclose you, but to focus you. Every surface is etched with planar sigils worn smooth by centuries of strain.

At the center of the chamber floats the keystone.

It hangs suspended in open air, a massive shard of rune-carved stone threaded through with pale light. The air around it ripples constantly, as though reality itself is being pulled tight and released in slow, painful breaths.

The sound here is wrong.
Not loud.
Not silent.
A deep, constant pressure, like standing too close to something immense.

This is not a place of power.

It is a place of containment.

IMMEDIATE PLAYER TAKEAWAYS

Make these clear without explanation:

  • The keystone is holding something together
  • It is under enormous strain
  • Removing or damaging it will change everything immediately
  • There is no clean way to do this

This is the decision the entire dungeon has been moving toward.

WHAT THE CHAMBER IS DOING (DM ONLY)

The Keystone Chamber is the last stable convergence point in Kethrys.

  • The keystone is actively resisting planar collapse
  • Every second here accelerates instability elsewhere
  • Once the keystone is disturbed, there is no pause button

This is the point of no return.

OPTIONAL DETAILS IF THE PARTY HESITATES

Reveal one at a time as needed.

  • Hair and loose fabric drift toward the keystone
  • Cracks spiderweb across the floor, then seal again
  • The light within the keystone pulses irregularly
  • Distant stone grinding grows louder and closer

If hesitation continues, advance the dungeon clock.

TOUCHING THE KEYSTONE

The first contact is unmistakable.

Describe:

  • Intense resistance, like pushing against a storm
  • A surge of heat or cold
  • The sense of something not wanting to be removed, but not alive

No saving throw yet. Let the moment land.

REMOVAL OR DESTRUCTION (TRANSITION MOMENT)

The instant the keystone is loosened, removed, or shattered:

  • The chamber lurches
  • The light fractures
  • The pressure snaps outward

Kethrys reacts.

This triggers:

  • Act III
  • Pursuit
  • Structural collapse
  • The awakening of the planar warden

Do not delay. Momentum takes over.

EXITING THE CHAMBER

Once the keystone is dealt with:

  • The chamber becomes unstable
  • Remaining is no longer an option
  • Escape is urgent and dangerous

There is no time to examine the aftermath.

SCENE END CONDITION

This scene ends when:

  • The keystone is removed or destroyed
  • The castle begins active collapse
  • Act III pursuit begins immediately

Do not allow rest or reflection here.

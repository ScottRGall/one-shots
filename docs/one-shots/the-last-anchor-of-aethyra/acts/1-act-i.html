1 Act I

The Approach and Forced Entry

Purpose:
Establish urgency, demonstrate that Kethrys is unstable, and get the party inside quickly without analysis paralysis.

Target Runtime:
20–30 minutes at the table.

SCENE SETUP (DM SUMMARY)

The party arrives in an open field beneath a fractured sky moments after being teleported by Thalos Aethyrian. In the distance, Kethrys flickers in and out of reality. Each phase shift carries it farther away.

The air hums. Gravity feels unreliable. The structure is already leaving.

This is the party’s only chance to enter.

VISUAL AND SENSORY CUES (USE 2–3, NOT ALL)

  • The castle appears solid, then translucent, then absent
  • Sections of stone overlap incorrectly when it phases
  • Wind pulls toward the structure, then abruptly reverses
  • Sound echoes out of sequence

Do not explain the planar mechanics. Let the scene show instability.

PLAYER OBJECTIVE

Get inside Kethrys before the entry point phases out.

There is no safe approach. Hesitation has consequences.

ENTRY MECHANIC (FORCED DECISION)

The entry point to Kethrys is unstable and only partially aligned.

Offer 2–3 clear approaches, for example (with suggested DCs):

  • Rush the opening as it stabilizes briefly (DC: 12, Check: Athletics or Acrobatics)
  • Use magic or tools to anchor themselves (DC: 14, Check: Athletics, Survival, or relevant tool use)
  • Split-second timing and coordination (DC: 12, Check: Group Dexterity or Intelligence check)

On failure: Minor damage or separation for one round inside, then regroup

Any plan can work. What matters is speed.

AUTOMATIC SUCCESS OPTION (IMPORTANT)

If the party proposes:

  • A clever combination of abilities
  • A resource cost they willingly accept
  • A dramatic, decisive plan

You may allow automatic success and advance the dungeon clock instead.

This reinforces the theme: success costs time.

PRINCIPLES FOR ACT I DCs

  • DCs should reinforce urgency, not punish creativity
  • Failure should cost time or position, not block progress
  • DCs should be suggested defaults, not mandatory rails
  • Never require more than one roll per character in Act I

If you break any of these, pacing dies.

RECOMMENDED DC FRAME (LEVEL 4)

Use this scale consistently:

  • DC 10: Straightforward, risky but obvious
  • DC 12: Coordinated effort, good timing
  • DC 14: Clever or magical solution
  • DC 16: Desperate or high-risk play

Avoid DCs above 16 here. The dungeon itself is the threat, not the doorway.

FAILURE AND COST (KEEP IT LIGHT)

Failure does not stop entry. It adds friction.

Possible costs:

  • Minor damage
  • One piece of equipment dropped or damaged
  • Party briefly separated but reunited immediately inside
  • The dungeon clock advances one segment

Never block entry entirely.

DUNGEON CLOCK INTRODUCTION

Once the party crosses the threshold:

  • Place the 6 visible tokens - the "dungeon clock" on the table
  • Explain that certain actions will advance it
  • Do not explain all triggers yet

The players should understand one thing:
time is the enemy

ACT I END CONDITION

Act I ends the moment:

  • The party fully enters Kethrys
  • The entrance collapses or phases away behind them

From this point forward, retreat is no longer trivial.

COMMON DM PITFALLS (AVOID THESE)

  • Over-describing the castle’s history
  • Letting the party debate optimal entry for too long
  • Running this as a combat encounter
  • Explaining planar rules up front

If discussion stalls, advance the clock and shift the structure.

DM MINDSET FOR ACT I

You are not testing skill.
You are testing decisiveness.

Kethrys does not wait.

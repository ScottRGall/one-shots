Archwizard Thalos Aethyrian

Planar Scholar, Last Warden of Aethyra

Role: Patron, truth-holder, damage mitigator
Alignment: Pragmatic Neutral
Presence: Calm, exhausted, precise
Vibe: A man who has already seen the cost of hesitation and refuses to repeat it

WHO THALOS IS (DM CONTEXT)

Thalos Aethyrian has been the party’s long-term patron. He is a respected archwizard whose reach extends beyond any single city or faction. His specialty is planar containment, not planar conquest.

The party trusts him implicitly. When Thalos says something must be done, it is because every other option has already failed.

He is powerful, but not omnipotent. What unsettles him is not danger, but inevitability.

WHAT THALOS KNOWS WITH CERTAINTY

(These facts are delivered in writing, not spoken)

  1. The castle cannot be stopped.
    Its transit across the planes is already underway. No remaining spell, ritual, or force can arrest it.
  2. One final anchor still exists within it.
    There were once six planar anchors. Five have already been destroyed. The last remains as a keystone buried deep within the structure. Alone, it cannot hold the castle in place.
  3. If the castle departs with the anchor intact, the damage will be catastrophic.
    As the castle phases out, the anchor will tear free, ripping a wound between planes that will spread across the region for miles.
  4. Removing the anchor does not prevent destruction. It contains it.
    If the anchor is removed or destroyed before transit completes, the rupture will still occur, but it will collapse inward. The damage will be localized to the castle’s exit point instead of scarring the land for generations.

If you are reading this, Kethrys has already begun its final transit.

There is no method remaining to stop it.

The structure you are approaching is a planar ruin once stabilized by six anchors. Five have already failed or been destroyed. The last remains in place as a keystone deep within Kethrys. Alone, it cannot hold the structure.

If Kethrys completes its departure with the keystone intact, the anchor will tear free as it phases out. The resulting rupture between planes will spread outward for miles, scarring the Material realm beyond recovery.

If the keystone is removed or destroyed before the transit completes, the rupture will still occur. It will collapse inward instead of outward. The damage will be confined to the castle’s exit point.

Retrieve the keystone if you are able. Destroy it only if you must. Be warned that it is powerful, unstable, and may resist interference.

Your survival matters. Nothing is gained if you are lost within Kethrys.

Time is already against you.

WHAT THALOS SAYS ALOUD

Thalos does not explain. He does not justify.

He hands the party a sealed letter, already warm with residual magic, and says only:

“You will not stop it. You will only decide how it leaves.”

-- Wait a short beat like you are thinking about explaining personally then before the party can interrupt --

“Read this. There is no time.”

The spell completes before questions can be asked.

WHAT THALOS WITHHOLDS

  • How long the castle will remain accessible
  • What guards the final anchor
  • Whether anything inside understands what is happening

Not because he is cruel. Because he does not know.

HOW TO ROLEPLAY THALOS

  • Never raise his voice
  • Never speculate aloud
  • Never promise success
  • Treat the party as capable adults, not heroes

He is not sending them to win. He is sending them to limit the damage.

DM INTRODUCTION CARD (GREEN-DM FRIENDLY)

The Last Anchor of Aethyra

DM Framing and Opening

Thalos Aethyrian has been your patron for years. When he summons you personally, it is never for routine work.

This time, he looks tired in a way you have never seen before.

He does not sit you down. He does not explain the situation. He presses a sealed letter into your hands and says:

“Read this on the way. There is no time to explain.”

With a single spell, the world folds.

Moments later, you are standing in an open field beneath a fractured sky. The air hums. Reality feels thin. In the distance, a ruined castle flickers in and out of existence, each phase carrying it farther from the world you know.

The letter in your hand details what Thalos knows with certainty. The castle is leaving. One final anchor remains inside. If it is not removed, the damage will be widespread and irreversible.

You cannot stop what is happening.

You can only decide how bad it will be.

The Scavengers

Planar Scavengers of Kethrys

Role: Moral friction, imperfect mirrors, optional combat
Disposition: Wary, desperate, pragmatic
Group Size: 3–4 scavengers

WHO THEY ARE (DM SNAPSHOT)

These scavengers entered Kethrys earlier, chasing relics and planar salvage. They did not understand the castle was in its final transit until it was too late.

They are not evil.
They are not brave.
They are trying to survive without walking away empty-handed.

They have seen rooms vanish.

They know time is running out.

HOW THEY ACT

  • Defensive, not aggressive
  • Weapons ready but not raised
  • Will bargain, lie, or threaten before fighting
  • Will flee if the fight turns against them

If attacked, they fight dirty and fast.

WHAT THEY WANT

  • To escape Kethrys alive
  • To keep at least one valuable item
  • To avoid drawing attention

They will not give up their prize easily, but they can be convinced.

SOCIAL LEVERS (USE INSTEAD OF COMBAT)

The scavengers respond to:

  • Clear leadership
  • Shared urgency
  • Promises of escape
  • Proof the party knows more than they do

They shut down if:

  • Threatened without leverage
  • Mocked or dismissed
  • Pressured while the castle shifts

If negotiation drags, advance the dungeon clock.

RUNNING THE FIGHT (IMPORTANT)

If combat breaks out:

  • Keep it short
  • Keep it loud
  • Advance the dungeon clock at least once

The scavengers:

  • Use cover
  • Retreat if bloodied
  • Do not fight to the death

A prolonged fight here is a failure state for everyone.

AFTERMATH CONSEQUENCES

If the party kills them:

  • Time is lost
  • Noise echoes through Kethrys
  • The castle reacts

If the party spares or escorts them:

  • Later complications
  • Hard choices during Act III
  • Narrative weight, not mechanical reward

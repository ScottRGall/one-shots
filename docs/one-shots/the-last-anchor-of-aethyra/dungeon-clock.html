Dungeon Clock

Dungeon Clock – Kethrys Transit

What This Is

The Dungeon Clock represents how much time Kethrys has left before it fully phases out of the Material realm.

It is a visible countdown, not a hidden timer.

When the clock reaches zero, Act III begins whether the party is ready or not.

Physical Setup (Recommended)

  • Use 6 physical tokens, ideally pie-shaped
  • Arrange them in a circle where all players can see
  • This is the clock

At the moment the party enters Kethrys:

  • Place all 6 tokens on the table

The clock begins immediately.

How the Clock Advances

Whenever the clock advances:

  • Remove one token
  • Do it openly
  • Do not explain unless asked

The visual loss is the point.

When to Advance the Clock

Advance the clock when the party:

  • Hesitates for too long in a scene
  • Engages in loud or prolonged combat
  • Lingers in a false refuge
  • Splits the party during a phase shift
  • Triggers major planar instability
  • Attempts to brute-force a gated path
  • Spends time arguing instead of acting

You do not need a roll to justify this.

What Each Token Represents

Each removed token means:

  • Fewer rooms remain accessible
  • Phase shifts become more violent
  • Mistakes carry heavier consequences
  • The Warden draws closer
